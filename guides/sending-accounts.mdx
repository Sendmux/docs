Every team can send email the moment it's created — no provider setup required. Sendmux gives each team a managed sending account called **Sendmux SES**, and you can add your own providers on top of it when you need more control or volume.

## Sendmux SES, the default account

**Sendmux SES** is a shared Amazon SES sending account that Sendmux provisions and manages for every team. It's active from the moment your team is created, so your first send works without any configuration.

| Property | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Availability | Provisioned automatically for every team |
| State on creation | Active |
| Starting limit | 500 emails per day |
| Limit reset | Daily, at 00:00 UTC |
| Used for | Every send, unless the send targets a specific delivery group |

Because Sendmux SES is managed for you, it's locked. You can **activate or deactivate** it, but you can't rename it, change its credentials, edit its daily limit, add it to a delivery group, or run a connection test against it. To send beyond the daily limit, request an increase rather than editing the account.

<Note>
  Mailboxes on the shared `@myagent.mx` address always send through Sendmux SES. To send through your own providers, use a [verified custom domain](/guides/domain-management).
</Note>

## Request a higher daily limit

When your usage for the day reaches **80%** of the daily limit, a **Request increase** button appears next to Sendmux SES on the **Accounts** page.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Accounts page">
    In the dashboard, go to **Accounts**. Sendmux SES is pinned at the top with a usage bar showing today's sends against your limit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Request increase">
    One click submits the request. Sendmux records your current limit and usage so the team reviewing it has the context — there's nothing to fill in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for the decision">
    You can have one open request at a time; the button shows **Request pending** until it's decided. Sendmux emails you the outcome. Approved increases apply as soon as they're granted, and the new limit shows on the Accounts page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Turn Sendmux SES off

If you'd rather send only through your own providers, deactivate Sendmux SES.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Deactivate">
    On the **Accounts** page, open the menu next to Sendmux SES and choose **Deactivate**. You can **Activate** it again from the same menu at any time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sends route to your own providers">
    While Sendmux SES is inactive, every send goes through your own active providers instead.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If Sendmux SES is inactive and you have no other active provider, sends fail with [`no_active_providers`](/sending-api/errors#no_active_providers) (HTTP 503). Reactivate Sendmux SES, or add and activate your own provider, to resume sending.
</Warning>

## Bring your own providers

Add your own SMTP or API-based sending providers under **Accounts** → **Add account**. Use them to:

- Send at higher volume than the Sendmux SES daily limit.
- Route specific traffic through a provider you control.
- Spread load across several providers with weighted delivery groups.

Group your providers into **delivery groups** and target a group per domain or per mailbox to override the default route. A send that targets a group uses that group's providers; everything else falls back to Sendmux SES while it's active.

One routing difference is worth knowing:

| Aspect | Sendmux SES | Your own providers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sending domain | Must be a [verified domain](/guides/domain-management) on your team | Any domain — your provider enforces its own sender policy |
| Daily limit | Managed by Sendmux (request increases) | Whatever you configure on the provider |
| Setup | None — provisioned automatically | You add and configure each one |
